What is ADRE?
Background & Context

The Assam Direct Recruitment Examination (ADRE) is the Government of Assam's centralised large-scale recruitment drive for Grade III and Grade IV posts across all state departments simultaneously. Conducted by ASSEB (formerly SEBA) under the State Level Recruitment Commission (SLRC), it is an offline OMR-based MCQ exam. ADRE 1.0 was held in 2022, ADRE 2.0 in 2024 filling 12,673 vacancies with appointment letters issued in January 2026. ADRE 3.0 is expected after the 2026 Assam Assembly Elections with an estimated 10,000–15,000 vacancies.

Top Strategy Tips
🏆
GK First, Always
Start with GK and Social Studies — memory-based, no calculations. Lock in easy marks before fatigue sets in from Maths and Reasoning.
📍
Assam GK Is Your Edge
Most aspirants neglect Assam-specific GK. Deep knowledge of Assam districts, rivers, history, culture, and government schemes is a major competitive advantage.
🎯
Accuracy Over Quantity
120 attempts at 90% accuracy = 105 correct = 102.5 marks. 145 attempts at 70% = 101.5 correct = 65.25 marks. Selective accuracy always wins.
📅
8-Week Study Plan
Weeks 1-2: Syllabus overview. Weeks 3-5: Subject deep-dive. Weeks 6-7: Daily MCQ practice (50/day). Week 8: Full mock tests only + light revision.
⚡
Maths Speed Tricks
Memorise SI/CI formulas, % change shortcuts, and ratio rules. For ADRE Maths, you have barely 1 minute per question. Speed matters more than depth.
📰
Daily Current Affairs
Spend 15 minutes daily on Assam Tribune or The Sentinel. Focus on Assam govt schemes, appointments, sports, science breakthroughs, and national awards.

ADRE History & Timeline
2021
Legal Framework Established
Assam Direct Recruitment Rules, 2021 enacted. SLRC formed to centralise hiring across all state departments.
2022
ADRE 1.0
First edition conducted. Established OMR-based MCQ format with 5 paper categories. Papers in Assamese, Bodo, English, Hindi.
July 2024
ADRE 2.0 Notified
12,673 vacancies notified. Bengali added as a paper language. Reading Comprehension (2 marks/Q) introduced for Degree level. Conducting body renamed SEBA to ASSEB.
Sep–Oct 2024
ADRE 2.0 Examinations
Exams: 15 Sep (HSSLC), 29 Sep (Degree), 27 Oct (Grade IV). Over 5 lakh candidates appeared.
January 2026
ADRE 2.0 Appointments
Appointment letters distributed to all 12,673 selected candidates on 9–10 January 2026.
2026
ADRE 3.0 — Expected
Notification expected after Assam Assembly Elections. Estimated 10,000–15,000 vacancies. Possible Prelim+Mains format under discussion.
