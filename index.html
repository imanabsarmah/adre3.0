What is ADRE?
Background & Context

The Assam Direct Recruitment Examination (ADRE) is the Government of Assam's centralised large-scale recruitment drive for Grade III and Grade IV posts across all state departments simultaneously. Administered by the State Level Recruitment Commission (SLRC) and conducted by ASSEB (formerly SEBA), the exam is an offline OMR-based MCQ test. ADRE 1.0 was held in 2022, ADRE 2.0 in 2024 filling 12,673 vacancies, and ADRE 3.0 is expected after the 2026 Assam Assembly Elections with an estimated 10,000–15,000 vacancies.

Top Preparation Tips
🏆
GK First, Every Time
Start with GK and Social Studies — memory-based, no calculations needed. Lock in easy marks before your brain gets tired on Maths/Reasoning.
📖
Assam GK is Your Edge
Most aspirants focus only on national GK. Deep Assam-specific knowledge (districts, rivers, history, culture, schemes) is your competitive advantage.
🎯
Accuracy Over Attempts
Attempting 120 with 90% accuracy outscores 145 with 70% accuracy. Never guess blindly. Selective accuracy wins ADRE.
🔁
Daily Revision Schedule
Week 1–2: Syllabus overview. Week 3–5: Subject deep-dive. Week 6–7: MCQ daily practice. Week 8: Full mock tests + revision only.
⚡
Maths Short Tricks
Memorise SI/CI formulas, %change shortcuts, and ratio rules. For ADRE maths, speed matters — 30 questions in 35 minutes means barely 1 minute each.
📰
Current Affairs
Read one Assam/national news source daily (Assam Tribune or The Sentinel). Focus on: government schemes, sports results, Assam-specific news, and science/tech developments.

ADRE History & Timeline
2021
Legal Framework
Assam Direct Recruitment Commission established under the Assam Direct Recruitment Rules, 2021. SLRC formed for centralised hiring across all state departments.
2022
ADRE 1.0
First ADRE exam conducted. Established the OMR-based MCQ format with 5 paper categories. Papers in Assamese, Bodo, English, Hindi.
Jul 2024
ADRE 2.0 Notified
12,673 Grade III and Grade IV vacancies notified by SLRC. Bengali added as a language. Reading Comprehension (2 marks/Q) introduced for Degree level.
Sep–Oct 2024
ADRE 2.0 Examinations
Exams held: 15 Sep (HSSLC level), 29 Sep (Degree level), 27 Oct (Grade IV). Conducting body renamed from SEBA to ASSEB.
Jan 2026
ADRE 2.0 Appointments
Appointment letters for all 12,673 selected candidates distributed on 9–10 January 2026.
2026
ADRE 3.0 — Expected
Notification expected after Assam Assembly Elections (Apr–May 2026). Estimated 10,000–15,000 vacancies. Possible Prelim+Mains format (unconfirmed).
